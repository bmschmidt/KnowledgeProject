BETIS, governor of Gaza under Darius, celebrated for his valour and loyalty. He defended a place of consequence with a few men against Alexander, who was there wounded in the shoulder, Betis thinking the Macedonian slain, returned triumphantly to the city; but in a second assault he was wounded and brought before Alexander, who cruelly ordered him to be put to death.
