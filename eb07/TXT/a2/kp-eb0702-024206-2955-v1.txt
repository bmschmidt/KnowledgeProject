AGDE, a city of France, in the department of Hérault. It is seated on the river Hérault, a mile and a quarter from its mouth, where it falls into the Gulf of Lyons, and where there is a fort built to guard its entrance. The greater part of the inhabitants are merchants or seamen. 'Γhe city is extended along the river, where it forms a little port wherein small craft may enter.
