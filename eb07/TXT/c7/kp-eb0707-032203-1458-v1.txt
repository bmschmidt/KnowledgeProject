CORD, or Chord, an assemblage of several threads of hemp, cabled or twisted together by means of a wheel. See Cordage. The word çomes from the Greek %oj⅜, which properly signifies an intestine or gut, of which cords may be made.

Cord of Wood, a certain quantity of wood for burning, so called because it was formerly measured with a cord. The dimensions of a statute cord of wood are eight feet in length, four feet in height, and four feet in breadth.
