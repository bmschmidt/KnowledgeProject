SYNALOEPHA, in Grammar, a contraction of syllables, performed principally by suppressing some vowel or diphthong at the end of a word, on account of another vowel or diphthong at the beginning of the next As, ilΓ ego, for ille ego, &c.

Conticuer' omnes intentiqu’ ora tenebant. Virgil. lt is called by the Latins collisio.
