CANEA, one of the circles into which the island of CaÏ€dia is divided, comprehending the western part of the island. Though hilly, the plains yield oil, flax, honey, corn, silk, and much fruit. The chief city, of the same name, is situated on the north const of the island, is fortified with strong walls and deep ditches, has several mosques and churches, and contains 4000 Turks, 3000 Greeks, and several Jew and Frank inhabitants. Among the latter are respectable mercantile houses, who carry on much commerce with Trieste, Venice, and other places, in oil, soap, and other articles. The harbour lias a mole, defended by a fort, but is only adapted to the reception of small vessels.

CANELLI, a town of Italy, in the province of Asti, of the kingdom of Sardinia, containing 3200 inhabitants, who produce some excellent wine.
