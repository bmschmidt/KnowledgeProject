CHARONDAS, a celebrated legislator of the Thurians, and a native of Catanea, in Sicily, flourished 446 years before Christ. He ordained that no person should appear armed in the public assemblies of the nation; but one day going thither in haste without thinking of his sword, he was no sooner made sensible of his mistake than he ran it through his body.
