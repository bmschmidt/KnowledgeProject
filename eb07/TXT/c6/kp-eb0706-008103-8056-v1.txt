CANICULUM, or Caniculus, in the Byzantine antiquities, a golden standish or ink vessel, decorated with preeious stones, in whieh was kept the sacred encaustum, or red ink, wherewith the emperors signed their deerecs, letters, &c. The word is by some derived from canis, or cαηiculus ; alluding to the figure of a dog whieh it represented, or rather beeause it was supported by the figures of dogs. The eanieulum was under the care of a particular officer of state.
