CATTI, a people of Germany, very widely diffused, reaching on the east to the river Sala, on the north to Westphalia, and oceupying, besides Hesse, the Wetterau, and part of the tract on the Rhine and on the banks of the river Lohne. The Hercynian forest began and ended in their country.

CATTTVELLAUNI, anciently a people of Britain, who occupied the country which is now divided into the counties of Hertford, Bedford, and Bucks. The name of this ancient British people is written in several different ways by Greek and Roman authors, by whom they are called Catti, Cassii, Catticulcani, Cattidudani, Catticludani, &c.
