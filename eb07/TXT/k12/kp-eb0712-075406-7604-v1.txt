KOHCRAAN, a district of Hindustan, in the northwestern quarter of the province of Lahore, situated between the thirty-third and thirty-fourth degrees of north latitude, on the western side of the Jhylum or Hydaspes River. It contains no town of note, and the face of the country is extremely hilly and wild, possessed by petty chiefs, who are tributary either to the Sciks or Afghans.
