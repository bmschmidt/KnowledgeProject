PALLA, in Roman antiquity, a mantle which women wore over the gown called stola. It was borne on the left shoulder, whence passing to the other side, under the right arm, the two ends were bound under the left arm, leaving the breast and the arm quite bare. It had a great many folds, and derived its name from παλλω, to shake or tremble. Palla Isle, a small island in the Eastern Seas, about six miles in circumference, situated to the south of Sangir. Long. 125. 30. E. Lat. 3. 5. N.
