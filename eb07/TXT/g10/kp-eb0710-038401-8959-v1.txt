GENTIAH, a small district of Hindustan, situated on the north-east of Bengal, bounded on the south by Sylhet, and on the north by the Garrow Mountains. The inhabitants are Hindus of the Brahmi□ical persuasion, and are governed by their own rajah, who is tributary to the Anglo-Indian government.

Gentiλh, the capital of the above district, lies without the pale of the Company’s territories, being situated in long. 91. 54. E. and lat. 25. 10. N.
