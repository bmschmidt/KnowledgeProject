BROUNCKER, or Brounker, William, lord viscount of Castle-Lyons, in Ireland, and the first president of the Royal Society, was the sonof Sir William Brounker, knight, and born about the year 1620. He was distinguished by his knowledge of the mathematics, and by the considerable posts of honour and profit he enjoyed after the restoration; for he had at the same time the office of chancellor to the queen and the keeping of her great seal, that of one of the commissioners of the navy, and master of St Catharineâ€™s Hospital, near the Tower of London. He wrote, 1. Experiments on the recoiling of Guns; 2. An algebraical paper upon the squaring of the Hyperbola; and several letters to Dr Usher, archbishop of Armagh. He died in 1684.
