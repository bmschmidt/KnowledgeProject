FRUMENTATION, in Roman antiquity, a largess of corn bestowed on the people. This practice of giving corn to the people was very ancient among the Romans, and frequently used to soothe the turbulent passions of the populace. At first the number of those to whom this largess was given was indeterminate, but it was fixed by Augustus at 200,000.
