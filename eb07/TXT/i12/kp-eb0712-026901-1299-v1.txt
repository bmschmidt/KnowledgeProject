INDUCTION, in Logic and Rhetoric, a consequence drawn from several propositions or principles first laid down; or a general inference deduced from a number of facts so arranged in the statement of them as to lead necessarily to the conclusion.

Induction, in Law, is putting a clerk or clergyman in possession of a benefice or living to which he is collated or presented.

Induction, in Philosophy. See Physics.
