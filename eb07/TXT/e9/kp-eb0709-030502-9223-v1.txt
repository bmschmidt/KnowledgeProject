EPHOD, in Jewish antiquity, a part of the priestly abit, being a kind of girdle, which, being brought from ehind the neck over the two shoulders, and hanging own before, was put across the stomach, then carried ound the waist, and made use of as a cincture to the unic. There were two sorts of ephods; one of plain linen or the priests, and the other embroidered for the high riest.
