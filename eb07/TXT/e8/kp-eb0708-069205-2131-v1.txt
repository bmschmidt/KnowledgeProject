EMOUY, Amoy, or Hiamen, an island of China, near the south-east coast, belonging to the province of Fokien. It was formerly resorted to by Europeans before their commerce was restricted to Canton. It is about fifteen miles in circuit, and has a fort constructed between the island and the main. Hcrc are several temples, one of them dedicated to the god Fo, with his statue of colossal size; and here also are the images of numerous other divinities, besides strange allegorical paintings. Long. 118. 22. E. Lat. 24. 30. N.
