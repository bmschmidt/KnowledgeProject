ECCLESIASTICUS, an apocryphal book, generally bound up with the Scriptures, and so called from its being read in the church, ecclesia, as a book of piety and instruction, but not of infallible authority. The author of this book was a Jew, called Jesus the son of Sirach. The Greeks call it the Wisdom of the Son of Sirach.
