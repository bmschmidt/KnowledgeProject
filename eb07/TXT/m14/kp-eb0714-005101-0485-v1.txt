MALACA, in Ancient Geography, surnamed Foederatorum by Pliny, a maritime town of Baetica (a Carthaginian colony according to Strabo), so called from malaclÎ¹, signifying salt; now Malaga, a port of Granada in Spain. Long. 4. 45. W. Lat. 36. 40. N.
