MELKSHAM, a town of the county of Wilts, in the hundred of the same name, ninety-nine miles from London, and situated on one of the great roads to Bath. It is a well [7:14:541] built town, chiefly inhabited by persons employed in making fine cloths and cassimeres. It has a cattle market, which is held on alternate Thursdays, but none for provisions is regularly held. The inhabitants were in 1801, 4030; in 1811,4110; in 1821,4765; andin 1831,4722.
