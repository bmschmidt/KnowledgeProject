CAeRE, in Ancient Geography, a town of Etruria, the royal residence of Mezentius. Its ancient name was Ar-gyllce. In Strabo’s time not the least vestige of it remained, except the baths called coeretana. From this town the Roman censor’s tables were called ccerites tabuloe.
