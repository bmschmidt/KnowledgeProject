REST the continuance of a body in the same place, or [7:19:188]its continual application or contiguity to the same parts of the ambient or contiguous bodies. It is therefore opposed ,to motion.

Rest, in Poetry, is a short pause of the voice in reading, being the same with the coesura, which, in Alexandrine verses, falls on the sixth syllable, but in verses of ten or eleven syllables, on the fourth.
