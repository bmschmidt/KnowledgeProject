AREA, in general, denotes any plam surface whereon we walk, &c. The word is Latin, importing more properly a threshing floor; and is derived from arere to be dry.

Area, in Geometry, denotes the superficial content of any figure. Thus, if a figure, e. g. a field, be in form of ’ a square, and its side be 40 feet long, its area is said to be 1600 square feet; or it contains 1600 little squares, each a foot every way.
