ANHOLT, a Danish island in the Cattegat, 32 miles north-east of Greenae, and 36 south-west from Lassoe. It is a barren spot, scarcely affording subsistence to 100 inhabitants, who from their language, which is Erse mixed with a few Danish words, are supposed to be emigrants /rom the Highlands of Scotland. Their chief pursuit is fishing. It is surrounded by dangerous banks of sand, for which reason a light-house is built on it, the occupation of whieh was a subject of contest with England during the war. It is in lat. 56. 32. 2. N. and long. 11. 49. 17. E.
