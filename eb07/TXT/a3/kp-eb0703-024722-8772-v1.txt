ANTHËSTERION, in ancient chronology, the sixth month of the Athenian year. It contained 29 days, and answered to the latter part of our November and begin-nĩng[7:3:248] of December. The Macedonians called it dasion or desion. It had its name from the festival antlιesteria kept in it.

, ANTHOLOGION, the title of the serviee-book used in the Greek ehurch. It is divided into 12 months, containing the offices sung throughout the year, on the festivals of our Saviour, the Virgin, and other remarkable saints.
