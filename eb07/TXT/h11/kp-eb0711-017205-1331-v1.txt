HAW, a sort of berry, the fruit of several species of mespilus, thence denominated hawthorns. Haw, amongst farriers, an excrescence resembling a gristle, growing under the nether eyelid and eye of a horse, which, if not timely removed, destroys it.

Haw, a small parcel of land, so called in Kent, as a HemphÎ±w, or Beanhaw, lying near the house, and enclosed for these uses. But Sir Edward Coke, in an ancient plea concerning Feversham in Kent, says hawes are houses.
