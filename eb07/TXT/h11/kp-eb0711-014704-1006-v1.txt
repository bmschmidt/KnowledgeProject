HARIOT, or Heriot, in Law, a due belonging to a lord at the death of his tenant, consisting of the best beast, either horse, or cow, or ox, which he had at the time of his death; and in some manors the best goods, piece of plate, &c. were called hariots.

Hariot, Thomas. See Harriot.
