VERSECZ, a royal free town in the county of Ternes, Hungary, 40 miles south of Temesv√°r. It is partly fortified and is the seat of a Greek bishop. Amongst its principal institutions are a high college for girls, a gymnasium, and a real school. Versecz is one of the principal wine-producing centres in the kingdom, its yearly export amounting to an average of five and a half million gallons. There is also a good trade in rice and silk. The population numbered 22.329 in 1880.
