NIJAR, a town of Spain, in the province of Almeria, 14 miles to the east-north-east of that town, occupies an exposed site on the southern slope of the Sierra Alhamilla. It has dye-works, and manufactures woollen stuffs and pottery; trade is carried on in the products of these industries, as well as in corn, cattle, barilla, and fine potterâ€™s clay procured in the neighbourhood. The population of the ayuntamiento in 1877 was 13,661.
