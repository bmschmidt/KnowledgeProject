SHEBOYGAN, a city of the United States, capital of Sheboygan county, Wisconsin, stands on Lake Michigan,[9:21:783] at the mouth of the river of the same name, 43 miles east of Fond du Lac and 52 miles north of Milwaukee. It possesses a good harbour, and, being surrounded by very productive agricultural land, exports annually a large quantity of grain. The manufactures include farming implements, enamelled hollow-ware, and stone-ware; there are a number of tanneries and breweries; and mineral water is exported. Settled in 1836, the city had in 1880 a population of 7314.
