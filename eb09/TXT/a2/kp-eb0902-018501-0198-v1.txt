APOLLINARIS, Sulpicius, a learned grammarian of Carthage, lived in the 2d century, under the Antonines. He is the reputed author of the poetical arguments prefixed to the comedies of Terence. He had for his pupils Helvius Pertinax, who afterwards became emperor, and Aulus Gellius, who speaks of the acquirements and character of his master in terms of the highest praise.
