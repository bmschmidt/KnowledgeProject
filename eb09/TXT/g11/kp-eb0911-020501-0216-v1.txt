GRIVEGNÉE, a town of Belgium in the arrondissement and province of Liége, is situated on the Ourthe about a mile and a half S. W. of Liege. There are coal-mines in the neighbourhood, and the town posesses iron foundries and manufactories of brass-wire, nails, and iron boats. The population is about 6000.
