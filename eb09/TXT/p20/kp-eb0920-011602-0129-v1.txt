PURPLE (πορφύρα), the name given by the ancients to a dye derived from various species of Murex and Purpura. (See Mollusca, vol. xvi. p. 648 sg ’.; Dyeing, vol. vii. p. 571; and Ph<enicia, vol. xviii. p. 804.) For the modern sources of the various shades of this colour, see Dyeing, vol. vii. p. 579.
