CARCAJENTE, a town of Spain, in the province of Valencia, 2 j miles from the right bank of the Jucar, iu 39Â° 4' N lat. and 3 31' W. long., with a station on the railway between Valencia and Almansa. It is inhabited chiefly by an agricultural population, but has a considerable manufacture of linen and woollen stuffs, and silk thread from the silk produced in the neighbourhood. The town-house (a modern building), the parish church, and the palace of the marquis of Calzada are its principal buildings. Roman remains have been found in the vicinity. Population, 8850.
