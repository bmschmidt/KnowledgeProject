JACOBÄBAD, a municipality and the chief town of the frontier district of Upper Sind, India, is situated in 28° 17' N. lat. and 68° 28' 45'' E. long. Laid out in 1847 by General John Jacob, on the site of the village of Khangarh, it is now the headquarters of the large military force of the Upper Sind frontier, and also of the local civil administration. It contains therefore a considerable European population, and possesses all the usual public offices and institutions of an important station. In addition to the cantonments, civil and judicial courts, dispensary, jail, post and telegraph offices, <fcc., it has also a “residency,” and lines for the accommodation of trade caravans (fâfîlas) from Central Asia. The civil court, which is under the Shikârpur jurisdiction, was established in 1870, the sessions judge of Shikârpur visiting it twice a year. Population, including the military camp, 10,954.
