BANGOR, a seaport and market-town of Ireland, county Down, on the south side of Belfast Lough, 12 miles E.N.E. of Belfast. It carries on a considerable trade in cotton and linen and embroidered muslin, and has a bank, a market-house, a parish church, several chapels, and a public library. It is greatly frequented as a bathing-place, especially by the people of Belfast. Remains of an ancient abbey, said to have been destroyed by the Danes in 820, are still to be seen. Population in 1871, 2560.
