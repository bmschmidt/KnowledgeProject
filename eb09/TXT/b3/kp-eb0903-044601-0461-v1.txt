BATU, a thickly-wooded island lying off the northwestern coast of Sumatra, 40 miles in length by 10 in average breadth, almost immediately under the equinoctial , line. Cocoa-nuts, oil, and trepang are exported. It is the seat of an active volcano. The inhabitants are a colony from the island of Nias.
