BEVERLEY, a seaport of Massachusetts in the United States, situated on a branch of Ann Harbour, and connected with Salem by a bridge built in 1788 It is 16 miles N.E. of Boston, on the Eastern Railway, and is connected with Gloucester by a branch line. The principal industry is the manufacture of shoes; and a considerable number of people are employed in the coasting trade and fisheries Population in 1870, 6507
