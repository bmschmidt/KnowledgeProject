BRONTE, a city of Sicily in the intendency of Catania. It stands in a healthy situation at the western foot of Mount Etna, on the river Giaretta, near a celebrated waterfall. It has considerable manufactures of linen and woollcu cloths, and some paper-mills. Good wine is produced in the neighbourhood. Bronte is of comparatively modern origin, having all been built since the 16th century. It gave the title of duke to Lord Nelson. Population, 14,589.
