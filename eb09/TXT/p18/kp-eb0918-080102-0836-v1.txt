PHOeBUS (φoιβoς, the bright or pure), a common epithet of Apollo (q.vf Artemis in like manner is called Phaebe, and in the Latin poets and their modern followers “Phoebus” and “Phaebe” are often used simply for the sun and the moon respectively.
