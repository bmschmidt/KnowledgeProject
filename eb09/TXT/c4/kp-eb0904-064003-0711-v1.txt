CAGLI (the ancient Calles), a walled town of Italy, iu the province of Pesaro e Urbino, at the confluence of the Cantiano and Busso, the former of which is crossed there by an ancient Roman bridge It is the seat of a bishop, and has a cathedral and several churches and monasteries, in one of which, Santo Domenico, is preserved a famous fresco by Giovanni Santi, the father of Raffaelle. The principal occupation of the town is the manufacture of leather. Population 10,213.
